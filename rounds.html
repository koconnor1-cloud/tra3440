Five Rounds — Scenarios & Modifiers

Round 1 Baseline Launch Complete
No scenario. Normal market conditions across all three routes. Round 1 is your benchmark — learn how your decisions affect load factor, cargo revenue, and profit before scenarios begin to shift the market.

All route parameters are at base values. There are no demand boosts, cost spikes, or disruption penalties. The only variables are the decisions you make.

Round 2 ATL Business Travel Rebound Complete
Corporate travel demand on PNS–ATL surged following a period of remote-work suppression. Business travelers returned to the Atlanta hub in higher numbers — conference activity, consulting travel, and financial sector movement all up.

Key effect: ATL base passengers rose from 72 to 85 per flight, and price sensitivity dropped from 1.3 to 1.0. Airlines could charge above the $185 reference fare with less demand penalty than usual. MCO and DFW were unchanged.

Round 3 DFW E-Commerce Cargo Surge Active — Due July 5
A major e-commerce retailer accelerated fulfillment through the Dallas–Fort Worth logistics corridor, driving a surge in regional air freight demand. DFW cargo handlers reported record freight volumes, and yield rates rose sharply as shippers competed for belly capacity.

Key effect: DFW cargo demand jumped from 14 to 19 units per flight, and cargo yield rose from $520 to $650 per unit. MCO leisure demand softened slightly (82→78 pax/flight). ATL returned to baseline — the Round 2 business rebound is over.

Round 4 Fuel Spike & Weather Disruption Upcoming — Due July 19
A perfect storm: jet fuel prices spiked following Gulf Coast refinery disruptions, while a multi-day severe weather system caused cascading delays across PNS, ATL, and MCO. Every airline takes a hit. The only variable is how much.

Key effect: Operating costs rise 8% on all routes. A $20,000 disruption penalty applies to every airline — but OC level determines how much of it you absorb. Passenger demand drops 5%, cargo demand drops 8%. No route-specific overrides — this hits everyone equally.

Round 5 Spirit Airlines Collapse — MCO Opportunity Upcoming — Due July 26
Spirit Airlines ceased all operations on May 2, 2026 following a second bankruptcy filing. Spirit had held the PNS–MCO pricing floor at $79. With that floor gone, displaced leisure travelers flooded the market seeking alternatives — and showed less price sensitivity than before.

Key effect: MCO base passengers jump to 98 per flight (from 82), market fare rises to $135 (from $115), and price sensitivity drops to 1.4 (from 1.8). Fuel costs normalize after the Round 4 spike. Strong round-wide demand boost across all routes.
