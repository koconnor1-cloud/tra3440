Your Airline

You manage a small regional airline based in Pensacola, FL. Your airline operates three routes and has exactly 30 weekly flights to allocate. Your strategy, airline name, and target market are locked at registration. Every other decision is yours to make each round.

Cost Leader
Lower costs, higher volume, thinner margins. Most resilient in disruptions. Less cargo pull.
Balanced Carrier
No special advantages or penalties. Steady and forgiving. Rewards consistent smart decisions.
Premium Service
Higher passenger demand and satisfaction, higher costs. Pricing discipline is critical.
Cargo-Focused
35% cargo demand boost. Lower pax demand. Rewards smart DFW routing and OC investment.
The Five Decisions
DecisionSet PerWhat It Does
Average Fare ($)RouteHigher fares reduce bookings on price-sensitive routes. Lower fares boost volume but may not cover costs.

Market Development (0–4)AirlineBoosts demand on all routes. Costs $0–$50K/week. Diminishing returns above level 2.
Operational Capability (0–4)AirlineImproves on-time %, cargo efficiency, satisfaction. Critical for Round 4 disruption protection.
Round 4 Warning

Round 4 is a disruption round. A fuel spike and severe weather event will hit all airlines simultaneously. Your Operational Capability level determines how much of the $20,000 disruption penalty you absorb. OC=0 means the full penalty. OC=3 reduces it by 55%. Invest in OC during Rounds 1–3 — you cannot protect yourself retroactively.
