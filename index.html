Your airline

You manage a small regional airline based in Pensacola, FL. Your airline operates three routes and has exactly 30 weekly flights to allocate. Your strategy, airline name, and target market are locked at the start and cannot change. Every other decision is yours to make each round.

Cost Leader
Lower costs, higher volume, thinner margins. Most resilient in disruptions. Less cargo appeal.
Balanced Carrier
No special advantages or penalties. Steady and forgiving. Good for mixed route strategies.
Premium Service
Higher passenger demand and satisfaction, higher costs. Pricing discipline is critical.
Cargo-Focused
35% cargo demand boost. Lower pax demand. Rewards smart DFW routing and OC investment.

Round 4 warning

Round 4 is a disruption round. A fuel spike and severe weather event will hit all airlines simultaneously. Your Operational Capability level determines how much of the $20,000 disruption penalty you absorb. OC=0 means the full penalty. OC=3 reduces it by 55%. Invest in OC during Rounds 1–3 — you cannot undo low investment once Round 4 hits.

Grading

The simulation accounts for 300 of 1,000 course points. Your final airline strategy report (200 pts) asks you to explain your decisions, interpret your results, and recommend what you would do differently. The leaderboard ranking contributes to participation points but the written analysis earns most of the grade.
